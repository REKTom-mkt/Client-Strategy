1 Bold Contrarian Strategy

Contrarian Play
Build a "Comfort Ride Care Network" for Healthcare Providers
Everyone in this market is chasing individual consumers through Google search. That's the obvious play, and it's increasingly expensive. The contrarian move: stop trying to win patients one at a time and build a B2B referral network instead.

Create a formal "Comfort Ride Care Network" with a dedicated landing page, one-pager PDF, and outreach program targeting discharge planners, social workers, dialysis center coordinators, oncology offices, and assisted living facility directors in Seminole and Orange counties. You're local, you know these communities. Offer them a dedicated booking line, priority scheduling, and real-time ride status updates (even a simple text notification system).

The Medicaid broker pathway is the highest-volume channel in NEMT (60-80% of trips for mature operations), but it comes with 60-120 day payment delays. The contrarian twist: target private-pay referral partners (assisted living facilities, concierge medicine practices, senior living communities in Winter Springs, Lake Mary, and Winter Park) where the patient or family pays directly. Higher margin, faster payment, more loyal customer base.

Why nobody's doing this: It requires in-person relationship building, not just SEO. Most NEMT operators are either tech-focused (Uber Health) or Medicaid-dependent. The middle ground of high-touch, B2B, private-pay referral networks is wide open.
Difficulty: Hard (requires sales effort)
Timeline: 3-6 months to build, 6-12 months to mature
Revenue Potential: Transformative
